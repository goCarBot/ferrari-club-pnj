## 3. FCA NATIONAL LOGOS

### Primary Logo: FCA Circular Badge

The FCA uses a **circular badge/seal** as its primary mark:

- **Outer Ring**: Split into two halves:
  - **Top half**: FCA Blue (`#041E42`) background with white text "FERRARI CLUB"
  - **Bottom half**: FCA Crimson (`#862633`) background with white text "OF AMERICA"
  - Thin white border separating the two halves
- **Inner Circle**: White background containing the **Ferrari Prancing Horse Shield**
  - Yellow/gold rectangular shield
  - Black prancing horse (Cavallino Rampante)
  - "Ferrari" script text below the horse on the shield
- **Outer Border**: Thin red/crimson ring around the entire badge

### Secondary Elements

- **"SINCE 1962"** text — appears below the logo in gold on the national site
- **Italian Tricolor Stripe** — green/white/red diagonal stripe pattern used as a decorative element (prominent on Penn-Jersey Region site)

### Logo Hierarchy

1. **FCA National Badge** (full circular seal) — Primary
2. **FCA + Region Name** — Regional chapters add their region name below the badge
3. **Penn-Jersey Region variant** — Uses the national badge with "PENN-JERSEY REGION" text below, sometimes with `www.fca-pjr.org` URL

### Important: Ferrari S.p.A. Prancing Horse Usage

The Prancing Horse (Cavallino Rampante) within the FCA logo is **authorized by Ferrari S.p.A.** as part of the factory-authorized club relationship established in 2010. It must always appear within the FCA circular badge context — never as a standalone element on regional materials.

## 4. FCA BRAND TONE

### Taglines & Key Phrases

| Phrase | Usage |
|--------|-------|
| **"The ultimate experience for Ferrari owners and enthusiasts since 1962"** | Primary tagline, hero area |
| **"Welcome to the world's largest and oldest factory authorized Ferrari Club"** | Secondary descriptor |
| **"Since 1962"** | Heritage marker, logo accompaniment |
| **"Cars, Culture, & Community"** | Penn-Jersey Region tagline |
| **"Join thousands of enthusiastic members across North America having fun every day"** | Membership appeal |

### Voice Characteristics

- **Prestigious but accessible**: Language conveys exclusivity while welcoming all enthusiasts
- **Heritage-forward**: Constant emphasis on "since 1962", "world's largest and oldest"
- **Factory-authorized legitimacy**: Repeated mention of the official Ferrari relationship
- **Community-centered**: Focus on shared passion rather than wealth/status
- **Activity-driven**: Emphasis on events, drives, concours, track days — things members *do*

### Tone Guidelines for Regional Use

- Avoid overly casual language (no slang, emojis, or informal tone)
- Always reference the FCA's national stature: "world's largest", "factory authorized"
- Use Italian automotive terminology naturally: Concorso, Cavallino, Prancing Horse
- Emphasize the **experience**: the cars, the community, the events — not the price/exclusivity
- Photography should feel aspirational but real — actual events, actual members, actual Ferraris

## 6. REGIONAL ALIGNMENT NOTES

### How Penn-Jersey Currently Aligns

The Penn-Jersey Region (fca-pennjersey.org) currently demonstrates **strong brand alignment**:

| Element | Alignment | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Logo | ✅ Strong | Uses the FCA circular badge with "Penn-Jersey Region" below |
| Colors | ⚠️ Partial | Uses a more vivid/orangish red rather than FCA Crimson; dark backgrounds |
| Typography | ⚠️ Partial | Uses bold sans-serif but not clearly Lato/Montserrat |
| Photography | ✅ Strong | Real event photography, Ferraris in scenic settings |
| Tone | ✅ Strong | "Cars, Culture, & Community" — great regional tagline |
| Italian elements | ✅ Strong | Green/white/red tricolor stripe prominently featured |

### Alignment Recommendations

1. **Adopt official FCA color palette**: Replace the vivid orange-red with FCA Crimson (`#862633`) for branded elements, and use PMS Red 485 (`#DA291C`) for CTAs
2. **Implement official fonts**: Switch to Lato (headings) and Montserrat (body) per FCA guidelines
3. **Maintain dark aesthetic**: The Penn-Jersey site's dark background works well and creates a premium feel — this is a good regional differentiation while staying on-brand
4. **FCA Blue integration**: Add FCA Blue (`#041E42`) to the palette for headers, section dividers, and secondary elements
5. **Preserve regional identity**: The "Cars, Culture, & Community" tagline and Italian tricolor stripe are effective regional signatures that complement the national brand
6. **Add "factory authorized" reference**: Include the "factory authorized Ferrari Club" language somewhere on the site to reinforce legitimacy

### Regional vs. National Branding Patterns

Comparing multiple FCA regional sites reveals **no enforced template**:

| Region | Platform | Design Quality | Brand Consistency |
|--------|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **National** | YourMembership CMS | Professional, dated | Gold standard |
| **Penn-Jersey** | Custom (Squarespace-like) | Modern, polished | Strong alignment |
| **Empire State** | WordPress | Blog-style, minimal | Weak alignment |
| **Southeast** | Various | Basic | Variable |

**Penn-Jersey has an opportunity** to be the most brand-aligned and visually polished regional site in the FCA network.

## 7. FERRARI BRAND ELEMENTS

### Ferrari Corporate Identity within FCA Context

| Element | Ferrari Official | FCA Usage | Regional Guidelines |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Rosso Corsa** | `#FF2800` (RGB 255, 40, 0) | NOT part of FCA official palette | May evoke in photography; don't use as brand color |
| **Pantone 185 C** | `#E4002B` (alternate Rosso Corsa ref) | Closest to PMS Red 485 (`#DA291C`) | CTA buttons, accent elements |
| **Prancing Horse** | Trademarked Ferrari S.p.A. | Used within FCA circular badge only | Never separate from FCA badge |
| **Yellow Shield** | Ferrari badge background | Appears in FCA badge center | Part of authorized logo only |
| **Italian Tricolor** | Heritage racing colors | Decorative stripe element | Great for regional flair |

### The Factory Relationship

In 2010, FCA's Board of Directors accepted Ferrari S.p.A.'s invitation to join the **International Council of Ferrari Clubs**. Key implications:

- FCA is officially recognized as the **factory-authorized** Ferrari club for North America
- Members receive benefits through **Ferrari North America** (10% parts/service at authorized dealers via "Bucks Back" program)
- The FCA logo has been evolving toward **Ferrari's World Club logo** standard
- The Prancing Horse usage within the FCA badge is **officially licensed**
- FCA regions must respect Ferrari's trademark guidelines when using any Ferrari branding elements

### Color Distinction: FCA vs. Ferrari

| | Ferrari Corporate | FCA Club |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Red** | Rosso Corsa `#FF2800` | FCA Crimson `#862633` |
| **Accent** | Ferrari Yellow `#FFD700` | FCA Gold `#EAAA00` |
| **Dark** | Ferrari Black | FCA Blue `#041E42` |

The FCA deliberately uses **darker, more muted** versions of Ferrari's colors to create its own identity while maintaining the Italian automotive heritage feel. The FCA Crimson is notably more burgundy/maroon than Ferrari's bright racing red.
