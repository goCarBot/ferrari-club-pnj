About the Festival

The story behind New Jersey's premier Ferrari fall celebration

Our History

The Garden State Ferrari Fall Festival began as a gathering of dedicated Ferrari enthusiasts from the New Jersey Chapter of the Ferrari Club of America Penn-Jersey Region. What started as a small autumn outing has grown into one of the region's most anticipated automotive events.

Each September, the festival brings together Ferrari owners, collectors, and admirers for a day of competition, camaraderie, and celebration. With certified IAC/PFA judging, the event honors both the art of Ferrari engineering and the passion of those who preserve it.

The festival embodies the spirit of the Garden State's autumn season — warm, welcoming, and rich in color — set against the backdrop of one of New Jersey's most beautiful venues.

TPC Jasna Polana

Nestled in the rolling countryside of Princeton, TPC Jasna Polana is one of New Jersey's most prestigious private golf clubs. Originally part of the estate of Johnson & Johnson heir J. Seward Johnson, the property's name — "bright meadow" in Polish — perfectly captures the sunlit expanses that serve as our show field.

The club's manicured grounds, elegant clubhouse, and stunning autumn foliage create an unrivaled setting for showcasing the world's most celebrated sports cars. From the moment you arrive, you're immersed in an atmosphere of refinement and natural beauty.

Our Parent Organization

Proudly part of one of the nation's most active Ferrari enthusiast regions.

The Garden State Ferrari Fall Festival is organized by the Ferrari Club of America Penn-Jersey Region, one of the largest and most active FCA regions in the country. The Penn-Jersey Region encompasses Pennsylvania, New Jersey, and Delaware, bringing together Ferrari owners and enthusiasts through drives, concours events, social gatherings, and educational programs.
